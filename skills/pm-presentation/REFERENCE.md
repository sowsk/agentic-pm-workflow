# PM Presentation Templates — Reference

---

## Template 1: Roadmap Review (Exec-Facing)

**Goal:** Align leadership on priorities, trade-offs, and what you need.
**Audience:** VP / Director / C-Suite
**Length:** 7–10 slides | **Time:** 20–30 min

| # | Slide Title (format) | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **[Product Area]: [Quarter] Roadmap** | Set context — team, timeframe, one-line north star |
| 2 | **Strategic context: why now** | Connect to company strategy / OKRs. 2–3 signals (market, customer, data) |
| 3 | **What we shipped: [Last Quarter] wins** | Outcomes, not output. Lead with metrics. |
| 4 | **What we learned** | Data, customer signal, hypothesis updates. Be honest. |
| 5 | **Now / Next / Later** | Visual roadmap. NOT a Gantt. Group by theme. |
| 6 | **Trade-offs we made** | What's NOT on the roadmap and why. Shows rigor. |
| 7 | **What we need** | Explicit asks: headcount, decisions, unblocks, budget |
| 8 | **Open questions / risks** | Flag the 2–3 biggest uncertainties |
| 9 | **Appendix** | Deep dives, data tables, technical details |

**Headline formulas:**
- "We're focused on [theme] because [evidence]"
- "[Metric] tells us [insight] → that's why [initiative] is #1"
- "We're not doing [X] in [Q] because [trade-off reasoning]"

## Template 2: Executive / Leadership Update

**Goal:** Keep leadership informed; surface decisions needed.
**Audience:** Direct manager, VP, skip-level
**Length:** 5–7 slides | **Time:** 10–15 min

| # | Slide Title | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **TL;DR: [1-sentence state of play]** | Bottom line up front — wins, risks, asks |
| 2 | **Wins this period** | 2–3 outcomes with data. Problem → decision → result. |
| 3 | **In progress** | What's being built, % confidence, ETA |
| 4 | **Blockers / risks** | Named blocker + 2 options + your recommendation |
| 5 | **Decisions needed** | Each ask: what, why it matters, deadline, your lean |
| 6 | **Next 2 weeks** | What you're leading (not just attending) |

**Rules:**
- Slide 1 is the entire update for a reader who only has 60 seconds
- Every blocker slide must include: options + recommendation + explicit ask
- No slide without a "so what"

## Template 3: Feature Pitch / Investment Ask

**Goal:** Get approval / resources / buy-in for a feature or initiative.
**Audience:** Leadership, cross-functional partners, or eng leadership
**Length:** 8–10 slides | **Time:** 20 min

| # | Slide Title | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **The opportunity: [headline]** | One sentence: problem + size + why now |
| 2 | **The problem (customer lens)** | Specific pain. Real quote or data point. Not hypothetical. |
| 3 | **Why this matters to the business** | Connect to revenue / retention / strategic bet |
| 4 | **Our proposed solution** | What it does. One visual. Not how it works — what it changes. |
| 5 | **Evidence it will work** | Research, analogues, early data, customer signal |
| 6 | **RICE / prioritization score** | Reach × Impact × Confidence ÷ Effort. Show inputs. |
| 7 | **What we need to build it** | Scope, team, timeline — specific, not vague |
| 8 | **Trade-offs and risks** | What we'd deprioritize. What could go wrong. |
| 9 | **Success metrics** | Leading + lagging indicators. How we'll know it worked. |
| 10 | **The ask** | Decision needed. Explicit. By when. |

## Template 4: Quarterly Business Review (QBR)

**Goal:** Review performance, align on learnings, set next quarter direction.
**Audience:** Leadership + cross-functional
**Length:** 12–15 slides | **Time:** 45–60 min

| # | Slide Title | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **[Quarter] in one slide** | Key metric, 1 win, 1 miss, 1 lesson |
| 2 | **OKR scorecard** | Red / yellow / green. No spin. |
| 3 | **Wins: what worked and why** | Outcomes with causal reasoning, not credit-taking |
| 4 | **Misses: what didn't and why** | Honest post-mortem. Hypothesis → what data showed |
| 5 | **Customer signal** | Top themes from research, support, sales calls |
| 6 | **Market / competitive update** | 2–3 signals that changed our thinking |
| 7 | **What we shipped** | Features → outcomes, not feature list |
| 8 | **What we didn't ship (and why)** | Transparency on trade-offs |
| 9 | **Next quarter priorities** | Top 3 bets with rationale |
| 10 | **Success metrics for next Q** | How we'll measure each bet |
| 11 | **Asks for next quarter** | Headcount, decisions, partnerships |
| 12 | **Open questions** | The 3 things we still don't know |

## Template 5: Strategy / Vision Deck

**Goal:** Align everyone on long-term direction and why it matters.
**Audience:** Leadership, company all-hands, or board
**Length:** 10–12 slides | **Time:** 30–45 min

| # | Slide Title | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **The world is changing: [insight]** | Open with a provocation or market shift |
| 2 | **The customer pain that matters most** | Specific, researched, compelling |
| 3 | **The opportunity we're uniquely positioned to capture** | Why us, why now |
| 4 | **Our vision: [one sentence]** | Aspirational but specific |
| 5 | **Strategic bets (3-year horizon)** | 3–4 pillars with rationale |
| 6 | **What success looks like in [year]** | Measurable outcomes |
| 7 | **How we get there: phased approach** | Now / Next / Later with decision gates |
| 8 | **Trade-offs and what we're not doing** | Honest constraint acknowledgment |
| 9 | **What we need to win** | Resources, capabilities, partnerships |
| 10 | **Why we'll win** | Differentiation, moat, proof points |
| 11 | **The ask** | Commitment needed from the audience |

## Template 6: PRD Readout / Alignment

**Goal:** Walk cross-functional team through spec and get alignment.
**Audience:** Eng, Design, Data, GTM, Legal
**Length:** 8 slides | **Time:** 30 min

| # | Slide Title | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Why we're building this** | Problem + business case in 2 sentences |
| 2 | **Who it's for** | Persona + job-to-be-done |
| 3 | **What success looks like** | Primary metric + guardrails |
| 4 | **The solution** | What it does (not how). Figma link. |
| 5 | **MVP scope** | In / out. Decisions made with rationale. |
| 6 | **Open questions by function** | Eng / Design / Data / GTM — what each team needs to resolve |
| 7 | **Timeline** | Milestones, not tasks |
| 8 | **Decisions needed today** | Explicit asks with options |

## Template 7: Customer-Facing Product Demo / Update

**Goal:** Show customers progress, build confidence, get feedback.
**Audience:** Customer stakeholders / champions
**Length:** 8–10 slides | **Time:** 30 min

| # | Slide Title | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Agenda + what we're here for** | Set expectations |
| 2 | **What we heard from you** | Mirror back their pain (show you listened) |
| 3 | **What we've built in response** | Frame around *their* problem, not your feature |
| 4 | **Demo / walkthrough** | Live or screenshots. Keep it focused on 1–2 key flows. |
| 5 | **What's coming next** | 1–2 near-term items with ETA |
| 6 | **What we're still figuring out** | Honest — invites input |
| 7 | **Questions for you** | 3 specific questions to get actionable feedback |
| 8 | **Next steps** | Who does what by when |

## Slide Headline Formulas

Use these patterns to write strong insight-driven headlines:

| Situation | Formula | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Sharing data | "[Metric] [changed] — because [reason]" | "Retention dropped 8% — our onboarding flow is the culprit" |
| Making a recommendation | "[Action] will [outcome] because [evidence]" | "Investing in X will reduce churn 12% based on cohort data" |
| Framing a problem | "[Persona] can't [do thing] without [missing piece]" | "Enterprise admins can't scale without bulk provisioning" |
| Stating a trade-off | "We chose [X] over [Y] to protect [goal]" | "We chose velocity over polish to hit the launch window" |
| Surfacing a risk | "[Risk] is real — here's our mitigation" | "API latency risk is real — we've staged the rollout" |

## Common PM Deck Mistakes to Fix

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Topic headlines ("Q1 Results") | Insight headlines ("Q1 proved retention is the growth lever") |
| Solution before problem | Lead with customer pain + business impact |
| Data without interpretation | Every chart needs a 1-sentence "so what" |
| Wall of bullets | Max 3 bullets or use a visual |
| Vague asks ("support needed") | Explicit asks ("Approve 2 eng headcount by Apr 30") |
| No narrative thread | Each slide sets up the next — check the flow |
| Jargon for exec audiences | Plain language — swap "north star metric" for "the one number that matters" |
| Features, not outcomes | Frame everything as "this changes [customer behavior] which drives [metric]" |
